The Challenge

The Fragmentation Problem

Companies outgrow their logistics infrastructure. Separate providers, separate portals, separate problems — zero unified visibility.

01

Provider & Mode Fragmentation

Managing separate ocean forwarders, air freight brokers, domestic truckers, and rail partners with zero unified visibility.

02

No Real-Time Visibility

Shipments go dark between ocean and inland. Air freight status lives in a portal. Trucking is a phone call. No single view.

03

Reactive Operations

Disruptions discovered after the fact. Missed vessels, customs holds, capacity crunches. Premium rates to recover instead of rerouting before impact.

04

Technology Debt & Data Silos

TMS, WMS, ERP, and carrier portals that don't talk to each other. Reporting is a weekend project. No single source of truth.

05

Talent & Bandwidth Constraints

Lean logistics teams stretched across ocean bookings, customs compliance, and carrier negotiations. Strategic thinking sacrificed to firefighting.

06

Compliance & Customs Risk

Cross-border moves create duty, tariff, and documentation exposure. Without expert support, delays and penalties become recurring costs.
